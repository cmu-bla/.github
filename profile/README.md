# Accounting AI Research Lab

**Computational Accounting Science**

Tepper School of Business · Carnegie Mellon University

---

The Accounting AI Research Lab (AARL) is the first university research
lab in the United States dedicated to computational accounting science.
We bring together accounting scholars, computer scientists, and industry
practitioners to formulate and solve open problems in accounting using
modern computational tools.

Our work rests on three ingredients: real-world problems and data from
the field, foundational accounting theory, and contemporary methods from
computer science and statistics. The lab carries forward an intellectual
lineage that includes William Cooper, Yuji Ijiri, and Shyam Sunder —
figures who shaped the management-science roots of accounting research
at Carnegie Mellon's Graduate School of Industrial Administration
(GSIA), the predecessor of today's Tepper School.

The lab operates across three integrated modes — research, education,
and industry collaboration — as a single unified effort.

## What's here

This GitHub organization hosts the lab's code and computational
artifacts. Most repositories are private to lab members and
collaborators; selected resources are open. For the lab's research
publications, members, activities, and the Ijiri Lecture Series, please
visit the lab website.
